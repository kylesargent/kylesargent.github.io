Kyle Sargent

I am an AI resident at Google Research, where I work in the Perception organization. While at Google, it has been my privilege to be mentored by Ce Liu, Deqing Sun and Huiwen Chang.

Previously, I received my undergraduate degree from Harvard. I earned an A.B. with a joint concentration in Computer Science and Mathematics and graduated Phi Beta Kappa and magna cum laude. I received Highest Honors for my senior thesis, Lipschitz Constraints in Wasserstein GANs, advised by Alexander Rush. I also worked as a research assistant at EvLab under Evelina Fedorenko.

Research

I am generally interested in research which lies at the intersection of machine learning and computer vision. I enjoy developing techniques that can produce stunning visual artifacts with practical uses. I also pursue research on enhancing the stability and robustness of models such as GANs and classifiers.
